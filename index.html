    What treatment can do for you

    Evidence-based benefits of
    hormone optimization.

    "Reduced hot-flash frequency & severity by 70–90% with appropriate MHT."
    Outcome benchmark · MHT
    Sleep

    Improved sleep

    Reduced night sweats and improved sleep continuity and quality.

    Cognition

    Mood & cognition

    Improved mood stability, reduced brain fog, better memory and focus.

    Skeletal

    Bone protection

    Reduces risk of osteoporosis and fracture — one of the strongest protective interventions available.

    Metabolic

    Metabolic health

    Improved insulin sensitivity, reduced risk of type-2 diabetes, body-composition support.

    Cardiovascular

    Cardiovascular health

    Started early — within 10 years of menopause or before age 60 — MHT is associated with lower coronary heart disease risk and all-cause mortality.

    Genitourinary

    Genitourinary & sexual health

    Relieves vaginal dryness, painful sex, urinary urgency and recurrent UTIs (genitourinary syndrome of menopause).

    Cardiovascular benefit reflects the SOGC "timing hypothesis" (Guideline No. 422e): it applies to early initiation and is not an indication to use MHT for heart-disease prevention.

    07 / 12Foundation Program: Menopause & Perimenopause Care
    Safety & risk information

    Our safety-first approach.

    We prioritize appropriate patient selection, evidence-informed prescribing, and ongoing monitoring. Each hormone — estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone — carries a distinct and manageable risk profile, which your NP reviews with you before any prescription is made.

    Hormone 01

    Estrogen Therapy (MHT)

    Considerations
    • Blood clots
    • Stroke risk varies by age, timing, and health profile
    • Breast tenderness, bloating, nausea, mood changes
    • Without adequate progesterone: endometrial risk
    01
    Hormone 02

    Progesterone / Progestins

    Considerations
    • Drowsiness or dizziness (often at bedtime dosing)
    • Mood sensitivity in some patients
    • Breakthrough bleeding or spotting
    • Formulation-dependent cardiovascular effects
    02
    Hormone 03

    Testosterone (if indicated)

    Considerations
    • Acne, increased body/facial hair
    • Scalp hair thinning
    • Voice deepening
    • Changes in lipids requiring monitoring
    03

    Findings outside the scope of this program

    Sometimes your results reveal
    something unexpected.

    Comprehensive bloodwork occasionally surfaces an incidental finding that falls outside hormone optimization — something that needs its own diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up.

    Example finding
    Hypothyroidism

    An out-of-range thyroid result (for example, an elevated TSH) needs proper assessment and ongoing management that sit outside this protocol's scope.

    What we do

    01
    We flag it clearly and notify you directly.
    02
    We refer you back to your family physician — or a walk-in clinic if you don't currently have one.
    03
    We send you with a full copy of your results, so the next clinician has everything they need to take it from here.

    When to seek urgent care

    Call 911 or go to the ER
    immediately if you experience:

    01

    Chest pain, shortness of breath, or coughing blood.

    02

    One-sided leg swelling or pain.

    03

    Sudden weakness or numbness, facial droop, severe headache, or speech changes.

    04

    Severe allergic reaction — swelling, hives, difficulty breathing.

    Non-emergency
    For any new or concerning symptom while on therapy that isn't an emergency, contact our clinic via secure message and we'll guide you on next steps.

    Lab work & optional add-ons

    Optional add-ons
    SHBG
    $69
    Sex-hormone binding globulin

    SHBG controls how much of your testosterone and estradiol is biologically active, so measuring it lets us calculate free (bioavailable) hormone levels — a more accurate read than total hormones alone.

    Vitamin D
    $40
    25-Hydroxy

    Low vitamin D is a common, correctable driver of fatigue, low mood and musculoskeletal aches that overlap with menopausal symptoms — so testing separates a treatable deficiency from hormone-driven complaints.
